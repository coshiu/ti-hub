Core Concept
Your judgment. AI's pattern-matching speed.
You load a chat with your JD, intake notes, and role context — then cross-reference candidate profiles against those criteria to get a structured fit estimate. The AI handles criteria-matching at volume. You bring everything that comes after: market context, the read on the person, the HM relationship, and the advocacy.

Think of it as a spell-checker for sourcing. It helps you move faster and think more systematically — but it doesn't make you a good recruiter.
"Amplify judgment, not replace it. The craft still matters — AI just gives you more room to master it."
⚡
Critical: Always open a fresh chat for each new role. Reusing a chat across roles lets prior context bleed into your evaluation and corrupts your signal.
Module 02

The 3-Step Workflow

Click each step to expand the full guidance and a copyable prompt. Run these in sequence for every new search.

01
Build Your Role Brief
Load all context before evaluating a single candidate
▾

Open a fresh chat for each new search. The quality of this setup directly determines the quality of every evaluation that follows.

→The full job description
→Key intake notes — especially anything from your HM conversation
→Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves, explicitly separated
→Why the role is open and what hasn't worked before
→Team culture, comp constraints, any HM-specific signals
Setup Prompt
"You're helping me evaluate candidates for [Role Title]. I'll share the JD and intake notes now. When I send profiles, assess each one against these criteria — flag top strengths, notable gaps, and anything you can't determine from what I've shared."
02
Cross-Reference Candidates
Paste the profile, get a structured fit breakdown
▾

Paste in a candidate's LinkedIn summary, resume text, or your own sourcer-written profile. The AI evaluates it against the loaded criteria and returns a structured read on strengths, gaps, and unknowns — fast.

Evaluation Prompt
"Here's [Candidate Name]'s background: [paste profile]. Using the criteria we discussed, how well do they match? List strengths, gaps, and anything you can't determine from what I've shared."
03
Use Output as a Hypothesis
Apply your judgment before advancing any candidate
▾

The AI's assessment is a starting point — not the conclusion. Use it to move faster through volume, then apply your own judgment. The AI doesn't know your HM the way you do.

Always Ask First
"Can I point to specific evidence in this candidate's profile that supports what the AI flagged?"
Sanity Check
"Before I advance this candidate to the HM — what's the strongest case against presenting them? What should I be prepared to defend?"
Module 03

Failure Modes — Where AI Gets It Wrong

The AI can be wrong in ways that look confident. Know these four failure modes before trusting any assessment. Click each for a deeper look.

◆
False Positives
Strong keyword overlap — but experience is surface-level or in the wrong context. AI flags a match your eye would dismiss.
→ expand
◆
False Negatives
Strong candidate underscored because their profile doesn't mirror JD language. Transferable value exists — AI misses it.
→ expand
◆
Hallucinated Details
Partial profile input — AI fills gaps with plausible-sounding but fabricated details. Always verify against the source.
→ expand
◆
Missing the Soft Stuff
Culture fit, trajectory, motivation. AI can prompt you to think about these — it cannot assess them. That's your work.
→ expand
Pattern Recognition
Confidence ≠ Accuracy
The most dangerous outputs are the ones that look thorough. A well-structured match assessment can still be built on a misread title, an inferred skill, or a missed nuance about what your HM actually wants. The output looking good is not the same as it being right.
Module 04

Fact-Check Checklist

Run through this after every AI assessment. Click each item to mark it complete. Reset between candidates — don't carry confidence forward.

0 / 6 verified
✦ Clear to Advance
Start Here Every Time
"Can I point to specific evidence in this profile that supports what the AI flagged?"
✓
Verify titles — scope, not just label
Confirm scope, team size, and actual decisions made. A "Manager" at one company is an IC at another.
✓
Trace the timeline — progression, not tenure
5 years at one company can span two very different functions. Look at trajectory, not just how long they stayed.
✓
Find evidence for every claimed strength
Point to where in the profile the skill is evidenced — project, scope, output. If you can't find it, it's unverified.
✓
Apply your HM knowledge before scoring
A "weak" AI match may be exactly what your HM wants. The AI doesn't know their quirks or past decisions.
✓
Return to the original document
Always reference the source resume or LinkedIn — not just what you pasted. You may have cut something that changes the read.
✓
Trust your pattern recognition
You have market context the AI doesn't. If a "strong" match feels off — dig before you advance.
⚠
Reset this checklist for every candidate. Don't carry over confidence from a prior profile. Each evaluation is independent.
Module 05

Prompt Library

Five ready-to-use prompts for every stage of the evaluation. Copy and adapt — never paste and trust without fact-checking.

SetupRole Brief — Chat Initialization
Use at the start of every search
"You're helping me evaluate candidates for [Role Title]. I'll share the JD and intake notes now. When I send profiles, assess each one against these criteria — flag top strengths, notable gaps, and anything you can't determine from what I've shared."
EvalCandidate Profile Assessment
Use for each candidate cross-reference
"Here's [Candidate Name]'s background: [paste profile]. Using the criteria we discussed, how well do they match? List strengths, gaps, and anything you can't determine from what I've shared."
ProbePressure-Testing a Flagged Gap
Use when AI surfaces a concern
"You flagged [specific gap] as a concern. What interview question would help me validate whether this is a real disqualifier — or whether there's transferable experience I might be missing?"
SanityPre-Advance Devil's Advocate
Use before sending anyone to the HM
"Before I advance this candidate to the HM — what's the strongest case against presenting them? What should I be prepared to defend?"
CompareShortlist Comparison
Use when choosing between finalists
"I have [N] candidates. Based on our criteria, compare them and explain the tradeoffs — don't just rank them. Where does each have the strongest fit, and where are the meaningful gaps?"
Module 06

Rules of the Road

These aren't suggestions. They're the floor that keeps your signal clean and your candidates treated with the care they deserve.

Do — Always
→Open a fresh chat for each role — no exceptions
→Load the full JD + intake notes before any candidate
→Verify every AI assessment against the source document
→Use AI output to sharpen your interview questions
→Apply your HM knowledge before advancing anyone
→Treat every match estimate as a hypothesis to test
Don't — Never
✕Advance a candidate based on AI match alone
✕Assume the AI caught everything — it misses soft signals
✕Paste partial profiles and trust the full assessment
✕Reuse the same chat across multiple roles
✕Skip fact-checking because output looks thorough
✕Let an AI score override your pattern recognition
The Mental Model
Scaffold. Not a Crutch.
AI accelerates the criteria-matching layer — the part that scales with volume. Your value is in everything that comes after: the conversation, the relationship, the read on the person, the advocacy you bring to the hiring team.
"The sourcing role has evolved into a strategic talent intelligence function. AI gives us speed — we owe it precision in return."
